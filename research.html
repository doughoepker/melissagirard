Research

I am currently completing a monograph that examines the rise and fall of popular women poets, such as Edna St. Vincent Millay, Sara Teasdale, Elinor Wylie, and Louise Bogan in the 1920s and ’30s. This work is supported by a 2015-2016 NEH Faculty Fellowship. I recently presented an overview of this project during a poster session at Democracy and the Humanities, a symposium commemorating the 50th anniversary of the founding of the NEH. You can download my poster here.

I have published essays on women’s poetry in JML, the Oxford Handbook of Modern and Contemporary American Poetry, and Poet Lore, and new essays are forthcoming in PMLA and the Cambridge History of Twentieth-Century Women Poets.
